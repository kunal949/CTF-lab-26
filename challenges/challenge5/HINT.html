🔒 The Core Logic: The "VIP Pass" Analogy

Imagine you are trying to enter a VIP club. To get in, the security guard requires a Digital ID Badge (which is our JSON Web Token, or JWT).

This badge is divided into three distinct sections:

  • The Instructions (Header): Tells the guard how to verify that the badge is real (e.g., "Check my holographic stamp").
  • The Details (Payload): Tells the guard who you are and your current clearance level (e.g., "Name: Student, Role: Regular Guest").
  • The Security Stamp (Signature): The actual cryptographic signature made by the club manager so no one can forge the data.

The Security Flaw (The Vulnerability)

Normally, if you take a pen and manually change your role from "Regular Guest" to "VIP Admin", the holographic stamp will no longer match your modifications, and the guard will reject the badge.

However, the verification gateway in this lab has a critical logic flaw: It reads your instructions first, and blindly trusts them without verification.

By updating the instruction algorithm (alg) to "none", you are explicitly telling the gateway: "This is a specific type of token that does not carry a signature block. Skip the signature check entirely."

Because the backend logic is vulnerable, it skips verification, parses your modified details, reads "role": "admin", and grants administrative context access.
